# Meetings and Ceremonies

Our recurring meetings and team ceremonies.

## Regular Meetings

### Daily Standup

**When:** Every weekday at 9:30 AM
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Attendees:** All {{TEAM_NAME}} team members
**Format:** Synchronous (Zoom)

**Purpose:**
- Share daily progress
- Identify blockers
- Coordinate work

**Structure:**
Each person shares:
1. What I did yesterday
2. What I'm doing today
3. Any blockers or help needed

**Tips:**
- Keep it brief (1-2 minutes per person)
- Focus on work, not details
- Take deeper discussions offline
- Async update in Slack if you can't attend

### Sprint Planning

**When:** Every other Monday at 10:00 AM
**Duration:** 2 hours
**Attendees:** {{TEAM_NAME}} team + Product Manager
**Format:** Synchronous (Zoom)

**Purpose:**
- Plan work for the next sprint (2 weeks)
- Estimate and commit to goals
- Clarify requirements

**Agenda:**
1. Review sprint goal (15 min)
2. Review backlog items (45 min)
3. Estimation (30 min)
4. Sprint commitment (15 min)
5. Q&A (15 min)

**Preparation:**
- Review backlog beforehand
- Come with questions
- Have estimation ready

**Outcomes:**
- Clear sprint goal
- Committed stories
- Estimates in Jira
- Team alignment

### Sprint Retrospective

**When:** Every other Friday at 3:00 PM
**Duration:** 1 hour
**Attendees:** {{TEAM_NAME}} team only
**Format:** Synchronous (Zoom)

**Purpose:**
- Reflect on the sprint
- Identify improvements
- Celebrate wins

**Format: Start-Stop-Continue**

1. **Start** - What should we start doing?
2. **Stop** - What should we stop doing?
3. **Continue** - What should we keep doing?

**Alternative Formats:**
- Mad-Sad-Glad
- Sailboat (wind and anchors)
- 4Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed for)

**Process:**
1. Set the stage (5 min)
2. Gather data (15 min)
3. Generate insights (20 min)
4. Decide actions (15 min)
5. Close (5 min)

**Action Items:**
- Must be specific and assignable
- Limited to 2-3 per sprint
- Tracked in next sprint
- Reviewed in next retro

**Rules:**
- Safe space - what's said in retro stays in retro
- Focus on process, not people
- Everyone participates
- Be constructive
- No managers (team only)

## Meeting Guidelines

### Scheduling Best Practices

**Do:**
- Check calendars before scheduling
- Send invites with clear agendas
- Respect focus time blocks
- Include Zoom links
- Set reminders
- Allow buffer time between meetings

**Don't:**
- Schedule back-to-back all day
- Book during lunch (12-1 PM)
- Book early mornings (<9 AM) without asking
- Book late evenings (>5 PM) without asking
- Schedule recurring meetings without regular review

### During Meetings

**Video Etiquette:**
- Camera on when possible
- Professional background
- Mute when not speaking
- Good lighting and audio

**Participation:**
- Be present (no multitasking)
- Contribute actively
- Listen actively
- Respect speaking time
- Use chat for links/notes

**Time Management:**
- Start on time
- End on time
- Use a timekeeper
- Park off-topic discussions
- Focus on agenda items

### After Meetings

**Required:**
- Share notes in Confluence
- Create action items in Jira
- Send summary to attendees
- Update relevant stakeholders

**Meeting Notes Template:**

```markdown
# [Meeting Title]

**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Attendees:** Names
**Facilitator:** Name
**Note-taker:** Name

## Agenda
1. Item 1
2. Item 2

## Discussion Notes
- Key point 1
- Key point 2

## Decisions
- Decision 1
- Decision 2

## Action Items
- [ ] Action item - Owner - Due date
- [ ] Action item - Owner - Due date

## Parking Lot
- Topics to address later

## Next Meeting
- Date/time
```

## Meeting-Free Time

### Focus Fridays

**What:** No internal meetings after 12 PM on Fridays
**Purpose:** Dedicated focus time for deep work

**Exceptions:**
- Customer meetings
- Urgent incidents
- External commitments

### Deep Work Blocks

Everyone should have at least:
- 2 hours of continuous focus time daily
- 1 full day of focus time weekly

**Protect Your Time:**
- Block calendar for focus time
- Use "Do Not Disturb" on Slack
- Decline optional meetings
- Batch meetings when possible

## Declining Meetings

**It's OK to decline if:**
- You're not needed
- There's no agenda
- It conflicts with focus time
- There's a better alternative (async)

**How to Decline:**
```
Thanks for the invite! I'm not sure I'm needed for this meeting.
Could you share the agenda? I'm happy to contribute async or join
just for the parts where my input is needed.
```
